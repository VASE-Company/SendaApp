format_quote

"The absence of color allows the texture of the city to speak. In the grey, we find the truth of the form."

The Narrative of Absence

This series explores the intersection of brutalist architecture and the fleeting nature of light. Captured over three months, the narrative follows the silent dialogue between concrete structures and the evolving shadows of the winter solstice.

Every frame is a study in texture, contrast, and the absolute absence of color. By removing the distraction of hue, the viewer is forced to confront the geometry of the urban environment. The project aims to reduce Berlin's modernist heritage to its most fundamental forms—lines, curves, and the deep, rich blacks of midday shadows.

Working exclusively with the Leica M11 Monochrom, the sensor's native ability to capture luminosity levels without an interpolating Bayer filter results in a clarity that mirrors the clinical honesty of the architecture itself.
